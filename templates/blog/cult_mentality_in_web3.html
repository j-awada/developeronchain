Cult mentality in web3

April 20225 min read

Maybe it's just me, but I don't like labels. It is ironic given that I "belong" to the LGBTQ+ community.

I guess labels can be useful to identify with a group. I don't have anything against people wanting to use labels. But I did recently see an interesting case of hypocrisy.

Personally, as a developer, the 2 blockchains I'm interested in are Bitcoin and Solana.

I went to a Bitcoin meetup where the keynote speaker had an art background and was sharing his wisdom on stage.

In this article, I don't want to only criticize, so I will say, he is a talented artist.

What I found notably contradicting is that his talk was centered around criticizing the "woke" culture in the US/Canada. How "woke" people belong to a free-speech cancelling cult. He shared his disappointement with the newwave comics that are being ruined by the introduction of characters such as women and lesbians.

He was clearly unhappy about the state of affairs.

But at the same time, he belonged to the cult of Bitcoin. Proudly mocking "shit coins". Pledging allegiance to the one and only BTC.

Yes, bitcoin maximalists are hypocryts. They are mad at the world for hindering free speech (and money) while at the same time being firmly against any use of other crypto coins.

I'm not saying you have to love other blockchains, but at least spend your keynote speech talking about bitcoin rather than shitting on everyone else who is not a bitcoin maximalist.

I have a general rule: If I am attending something like a talk where I start having negative emotions provoked by the speaker like anger, hate, etc then I'm in the wrong place and I need to leave.

I went to that bitcoin meetup wanting to learn more about the chain, meet techie people and joyously discuss ideas.. But instead I got the same kind of "woke" rhetoric except from the far right.

As Paul Graham's article title accurately says, keep your identity small.
